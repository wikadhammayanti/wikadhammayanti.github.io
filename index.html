Total Sales by User with Sales Decrease Filter

This report describes a SQL query that was used to retrieve total sales data for users in a flight dataset, with a filter applied to identify users with sales that decreased by a certain amount each year. The query was constructed using several clauses, including SELECT, FROM, INNER JOIN, GROUP BY, and HAVING. The SELECT clause was used to retrieve the user ID and name, as well as the total sales data for each year from 2020 to 2023. The FROM clause specified the flight dataset table, and the INNER JOIN clause was used to join the user dataset table to retrieve user names. The GROUP BY clause was used to group the data by user ID and name. Finally, the HAVING clause was used to filter the data to identify users with sales that decreased by at least 10,000 each year. The query returned a list of users who met the specified criteria, along with their total sales data for each year. The list was ordered by user ID. Based on the results of this query, it is recommended that further investigation be conducted into the sales trends for the users identified by the filter. This may involve examining factors such as pricing, competition, and customer behavior to determine the cause of the sales decrease. It may also be helpful to conduct additional queries or analyses to gain more insight into the data. In conclusion, the SQL query described in this report was used to retrieve total sales data for users in a flight dataset, with a filter applied to identify users with sales that decreased by a certain amount each year. The results of the query can be used to inform further analysis and decision-making related to the sales trends for these users.

    Description:

  • Conducted data exploration, visualization, and statistical analysis to draw meaningful conclusions from the dataset.

    • Key responsibilities and Achievement:

  • Utilized Jupyter Notebook to import, clean, and preprocess the HR dataset for analysis.
  • Conducted a correlation, suggesting that as the salary increases, there is a slight increase in performance score.
  • Created visualization, including scatter plots, to explore the relationship between salary and performance scores, which indicated the presence of heteroscedasticity, suggesting that the results may not be reliable for prediction.
  • Performed gender- based analysis, finding that, on average, female employees have a higher performance scored compared to male employees. However, the analysis also revealed performance differences based on marital status.
  • Examined the performance score of married, divorced, widowed, separated, and single employees based on gender, identifying variations in average performance scores for different marital statuses and gender combinations.
  • Provided recommendations based on the findings, suggesting that the company should investigate and understand the factors associated with marital status that may influence performance differently based on gender. This can help reduce biases and promote a fair and equal working environment.
  • Recommended optimizing the company's recruitment strategy by focusing on sources such as LinkedIn, and Google search, which were identified as effective in attracting top talent. Additionally, encouraging employee referrals and increasing the referral bonus can attract more high- quality referrals. Attending diversity job fairs can also expand the pool of diverse candidates.

    • Benefit and Impact:

  • Generated valuable insights from the HR dataset, providing actionable recommendations for the company to improved their recruitment strategy and promote diversity and equality in the workplace.
